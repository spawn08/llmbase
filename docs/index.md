# LLMBase

> A living reference for engineers entering the world of Large Language Models. From first principles to cutting-edge research — with visualizations, code, and interview-ready depth.

Welcome to **LLMBase**. This guide is designed to take you from the fundamental mathematics of language modeling all the way to building, serving, and understanding state-of-the-art Large Language Models.

## Who is this for?

- **Software Engineers** looking to transition into AI/ML roles.
- **Candidates preparing for System Design and ML Interviews**.
- **Researchers** who need quick, runnable implementations of core architectures.

## Prerequisites

To get the most out of LLMBase, you should have:

- **Python Proficiency**: Comfortable with Python 3.11+, OOP, and basic functional programming.
- **Basic Calculus & Linear Algebra**: Understanding of derivatives, matrix multiplication, and vectors.
- **Familiarity with PyTorch**: Basic tensor operations and neural network modules (`torch.nn`).

## How to Use This Guide

LLMBase is split into 7 progressive parts:

1. **[Foundations](01_foundations/index.md)**: The math and theory before the Transformer.
2. **[Core Architectures](02_core_architectures/index.md)**: Transformers, GPT, BERT, T5, MoE, and Mamba.
3. **[Training & Alignment](03_training/index.md)**: Pre-training, SFT, RLHF, DPO, and PEFT.
4. **[Inference & Serving](04_inference/index.md)**: Decoding, KV Cache, vLLM, and Quantization.
5. **[Advanced Topics](05_advanced/index.md)**: RAG, Vector DBs, Agents, Multimodal, and Prompt Engineering.
6. **[Top 25 Research Papers](06_research_papers/index.md)**: Summaries, code, and interview takeaways for landmark papers.
7. **[Recent Advances](07_recent_advances/index.md)**: A rolling log of the frontier (DeepSeek R1, Llama 3.1, etc.).

Each section contains intuitive explanations, architecture diagrams, mathematical formulations, and runnable code snippets. 

Let's dive in!
